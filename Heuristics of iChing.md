### 1. Heuristics of the Structural Mechanism (The Line and Hexagram) üìè

These define the required geometric and symbolic composition that allows the system to represent the universe.

| Heuristic | Axiomatic Statement | Violation Creates... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C1: The Binary Foundation** | All states of change are generated from the **dynamic interplay of two fundamental, opposite forces** ($\text{Yin}$ - broken line, $\text{Yang}$ - solid line). All complexity arises from this binary pair. | **Multi-State System:** A system that relies on three or more fundamental symbols, diluting the clear, primal polarity of opposition and completion. |
| **C2: The Principle of Six-Fold Recursion** | The total state of a situation is defined by the **combination and layering of six lines** (Hexagram), creating a definitive, yet cyclically related, set of 64 possibilities ($\text{2}^6 = 64$). | **Unbounded Chaos:** A system with an indefinite or excessively large number of states (e.g., 100+ lines), making systematic pattern recognition and interpretation impossible. |
| **C3: The Dynamic Pivot (Moving Lines)** | The forecast is not static; specific lines within the Hexagram must be designated as "moving" (changing from $\text{Yin}$ to $\text{Yang}$ or vice versa), indicating the **imminent, active force of transformation** currently at work. | **Static Snapshot:** A reading that provides only a single, fixed meaning without indicating the inherent direction of change, rendering the advice descriptive but not prescriptive. |

### 2. Heuristics of Predictive Context (The Relation) üß©

These define how the generated symbols relate to each other and to the querent's question.

| Heuristic | Axiomatic Statement | Violation Creates... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C4: The Primary and Secondary State** | The reading must consist of two Hexagrams: the **Hexagram of the Present** (the initial state) and the **Hexagram of the Future** (the state resulting from the movement of the Pivot lines $\text{C3}$). | **Mono-State Diagnosis:** A system that offers only a single symbolic outcome, failing to illustrate the transitional path and the required intervening action. |
| **C5: The Core Context (Nuclear Trigrams)** | The intermediate, non-explicit state of the situation must be discernible by extracting the **central four lines** of the Primary Hexagram to form two hidden (nuclear) Trigrams, providing a deeper internal context. | **Surface-Level Analysis:** A system that only interprets the whole, external structure, missing the underlying, hidden dynamics and internal tensions within the situation. |
| **C6: The Interplay of Tensions** | Interpretation must focus on the **relationship between the two Hexagrams** ($\text{Present} \rightarrow \text{Future}$), identifying the obstacles, assistance, and required balance necessary to navigate the transition. | **Isolated Interpretation:** Treating the two Hexagrams as unrelated events, losing the I Ching's core wisdom that the future state is a direct, logical evolution of the present. |

### 3. Heuristics of Ethical Utility (The Advice) üôè

These define the required nature of the guidance derived from the system.

| Heuristic | Axiomatic Statement | Violation Creates... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C7: Alignment with Natural Law** | The advice must consistently align the querent's actions with the **natural, flowing, and moral order** ($\text{Tao}$). The purpose is not mere personal gain, but harmonious engagement with reality. | **Self-Serving Prediction:** A system that purely advises on maximizing short-term, amoral gain, disregarding long-term consequence or ethical alignment. |
| **C8: Active, Timely Non-Intervention** | The guidance often dictates that the **most correct action is non-action** (patience, withdrawal, or receptive waiting), emphasizing the importance of timing ($\text{Kairos}$) over forceful exertion. | **Constant Force Mandate:** A system that always advises aggressive intervention and continuous struggle, ignoring the strategic necessity of stillness and receptive power. |

## The Euclidean Analogy: New Systems of Change

A classic I Ching consultation adheres to all eight of these axioms, providing deep, layered, and ethical advice on transformation. Removing one defines a new symbolic or predictive system:

* **Violate C1 (The Binary Foundation):** You define **Alchemy or Chemistry**. The foundation requires multiple distinct inputs (elements, compounds) beyond a simple $\text{Yin}$/$\text{Yang}$ duality to describe a state.
* **Violate C4 (The Primary and Secondary State):** You define **Fortune Telling**. The system provides a single, fixed future state without detailing the steps or the present context required to move there.
* **Violate C7 (Alignment with Natural Law):** You define **Machiavellian Strategy**. The system provides guidance purely on how to manipulate the present situation to achieve a desired outcome, without regard for moral or natural law.
